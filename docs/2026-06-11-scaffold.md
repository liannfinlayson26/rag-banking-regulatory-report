## Decisions
- **Table parser: pdfplumber + Camelot.** Banking regulatory filings are
  dominated by *bordered* (fully-ruled) tables. Camelot's `lattice` flavor
  detects ruling lines directly, producing cleaner cell boundaries than
  text-clustering. Chose Camelot over `unstructured` because the latter is
  heavier (downloads ML models) and less precise on dense financial tables.
  pdfplumber stays as the primary text/geometry extractor and handles
  borderless tables.
- **camelot-py 2.0.0** no longer exposes the `[cv]` extra — OpenCV
  (`opencv-python-headless`) is now a core dependency. Requires the Ghostscript
  system binary (already present at `/opt/homebrew/bin/gs`).
